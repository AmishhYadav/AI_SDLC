1. Executive Summary

The Domain Architecture Specification (DAS) establishes the foundational architecture of the Enterprise AI Delivery Platform. It defines the platform's business domains, architectural principles, ownership boundaries, communication patterns, and enterprise design philosophy.

Unlike the Business Requirements Document (BRD), which defines what the platform must achieve, the DAS defines how responsibilities are organized across the platform while remaining independent of implementation technologies.

The DAS serves as the authoritative reference for:

High-Level Design (HLD)

Service Architecture

LangGraph Architecture

Entity Relationship Diagram (ERD)

API Contracts

Event-Driven Architecture

RBAC

Team Ownership

2. Objectives

The Domain Architecture shall:

Clearly define bounded contexts.

Establish ownership of business capabilities.

Define communication principles between domains.

Separate business concerns from implementation concerns.

Enable independent scalability of domains.

Support future migration from a modular monolith to microservices.

Establish AI ownership boundaries.

Define shared enterprise assets.

4. Architectural Principles

AP-01 Business-Driven Design

Business capabilities determine domain boundaries.

AP-02 Single Ownership

Every business object shall have exactly one owning domain.

AP-03 Loose Coupling

Domains shall communicate using events and published interfaces.

AP-04 Independent Evolution

Domains should evolve independently whenever possible.

AP-05 AI as Infrastructure

Business domains consume AI capabilities.

Only the AI Platform Domain owns AI execution infrastructure.

AP-06 Living Knowledge

Knowledge generated by the platform is considered a permanent enterprise asset.

AP-07 Human Governance

Critical engineering decisions require human approval.

AP-08 Documentation First

Implementation follows approved documentation.
